Contribute

Contributions from the community are the backbone of open source projects. We welcome all individuals who wish to help develop or provide support for the MOEA Framework.

Requirements

In order to maintain a quality product, it is necessary to mandate certain requirements on source code contributions (including associated data files and other resources).

  • All contributions must adhere to and be licensed under the GNU Lesser General Public License (GNU LGPL), version 3 or later. The contributor must also ensure that any individual, school or business which may own the rights to any part of the contribution agrees to license under the GNU LGPL.
  • All contributions must be fully documented. As the MOEA Framework is academic in nature, the documentation should include citations to the original works describing the contribution.
  • The contribution must have academic value. This means that preference is given to works which are published in scientific, peer-reviewed literature.
  • Whenever possible, the contribution should be fully tested using unit tests. Tests should cover all non-trivial code whose correctness is not immediately obvious.
  • The implementation should follow software best practices, favor clear and understandable source code, and follow the general formatting and structure of existing code.

Submission Process

A submission process is in place to ensure all requirements are satisfied. The submission process begins when the contributor submits a patch file to admin@moeaframework.org. In addition, the contributor should declare all copyright holders and any patents covering the contribution.

One of the core project developers will be assigned to handle the submission. The developer will verify that the requirements are satisfied, or notify the contributor of what changes are required. Minor changes will likely be fixed internally. If there are copyright and patent concerns, the developer may ask the contributor to obtain and submit a signed document by the individuals, school or business who own the rights to the contribution. This is a necessary step to ensure the contribution is legally conveyed under the terms of the GNU LGPL.

Upon acceptance, the contribution will be merged into the MOEA Framework or one of its extensions. We ask that contributors be available in the case that additional support is needed during this process.

Where to Start

The TODO document distributed with the source code is an excellent starting point for individuals looking for ways to contribute. Also check the bug tracker and feature request links on the support page.
